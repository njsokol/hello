Passions

Throughout my career I have found a passion through building web experiences. From the front end, to the backend, and design, to support/SRE, the lifecycle of a product is more than deliverables and deadlines. As an experience, we can find shared patterns, reusability, synchronization, and deeper meaning to our work and the interactions of customers.

"We reserve the right to get better."

We shouldn't count failure as a bad thing. We also shouldn't fill our packs so full we can't carry it. Get our designs/prototypes/mvps (whatever you want to call it) into the hands of real users and make decisions based off data.

"Design for users. Why anything else?"

Duh. If we aren't building a user-centric design - are we building for robots? Or do we just like the guessing game?

"Make things as small as they can be. Then make them bigger."

Iterate, iterate, iterate some more. Design is never done, and we shouldn't consider our products ever "fully complete". We should start small, fail fast, recovery even faster, and keep our debt in check.

A few of my favorite things...

  • Leading effective teams.
  • Building things with JavaScript.
  • Mobile friendly + Accessibility
  • Designing great user experiences.
  • Inclusive design and diversity.
  • Being unique.

Craft

I am an active learner and find doing something the best way to understand it. I never stop exploring new technologies or walking different paths (why choose one?). I also enjoy pushing the boundaries of what is possible; that includes performance, IE support, and user experience.

My greatest fear is that everything becomes the same. Once the robots take over, haha - but seriously, the web will become a less enjoyful experience. The web should be celebrated with new technologies, frameworks, themes, designs, and languages. It should be used for expression, like an artist, describing a history, legacy or future.

Be Kind, Rewind

Thank God for Blockbuster. Life moves at an incredibly fast pace. If we don't make the time to slow down once in a while and enjoy it and everything we have accomplished thus far - what are we doing? I enjoying learning about the web, and being half a ux designer. I don't see myself ever stopping, because I love to do it.
